Doomscroll

Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2025

Doomscroll is a screen time reducer for Android. This policy explains how the app handles your data. The short version: everything stays on your device. We collect nothing, share nothing, and have no servers.

Data We Collect

None. Doomscroll does not collect, transmit, or store any personal data on external servers. There is no account, no sign-in, and no backend infrastructure of any kind.

Data Stored On Your Device

Doomscroll stores the following data locally in your device's private app storage, inaccessible to other apps:

  • The list of apps you choose to monitor
  • A log of interception events (when you were intercepted, what you chose)
  • Focus session records (goal, duration, start/end times)
  • App configuration (daily bypass limits, time block windows)

This data never leaves your device and is deleted when you uninstall the app.

Permissions Used

Accessibility Service

Doomscroll uses Android's Accessibility Service solely to detect when a monitored app comes to the foreground, so it can display the interception screen. It does not read screen content, capture keystrokes, access personal data, or interact with other apps in any other way.

Usage Access (Usage Stats)

Used to display per-app screen time on the home screen. This data is read locally and never transmitted anywhere.

Notifications

Used to show a countdown notification during active Focus Mode sessions. Notifications are generated locally; no external service is involved.

Internet

Used only to check for app updates by fetching a small version manifest file from GitHub. No personal data is included in this request.

Third-Party Services

Doomscroll does not integrate any analytics, advertising, crash reporting, or third-party SDKs that transmit data off-device.

Children's Privacy

Doomscroll is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect any information from anyone.

Changes to This Policy
